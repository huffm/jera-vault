# Voice And Tone

## Tone

The Jera Technologies voice should be calm, senior, practical, and product minded.

Write like a serious software engineering company. The language should be clear enough for business readers and specific enough for technical readers.

## Style Principles

- Prefer concrete software outcomes over vague AI claims.
- Explain systems in terms of workflows, decisions, data, automation, and review.
- Keep public copy concise and direct.
- Use proper casing for products, services, and company names.
- Avoid inflated agency language.
- Avoid hype around AI.
- Avoid guarantees about model accuracy or business outcomes.

## Public Copy Pattern

Good public copy should explain:

1. What Jera Technologies builds.
2. Who the work helps.
3. What kind of workflow or decision it improves.
4. Why the product portfolio demonstrates engineering capability.

## Example Tone

Jera Technologies builds applied AI software that turns complex workflows into structured, reviewable systems. The work focuses on practical products, automation, integrations, and decision support.

## Avoid

Do not write like a personal portfolio, a casual studio, or a speculative AI showcase. The company should sound focused, credible, and ready for serious software work.

## Current Website Tone

The first site slice uses a calm, senior, product minded tone. Public pages avoid hype language, avoid casual portfolio framing, and use business facing language around workflows, modernization, automation, decision support, and applied AI software.

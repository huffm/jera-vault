# Truth Boundaries

This note defines what Jera Technologies can and cannot claim publicly.

## Allowed Claims

- Jera Technologies builds software products and business solutions.
- DAI is a Jera Technologies product focused on structured AI workflows.
- The matchup analyzer is a Jera built decision support application.
- Jera Technologies can describe owned products as internal products, Jera built products, solution examples, or product examples.
- Jera Technologies can discuss high level engineering patterns used across products.
- Malcolm can reference Jera Technologies from LinkedIn as Founder / Principal Software Engineer.

## Claims To Avoid

- Do not imply paid client work unless explicitly provided later.
- Do not describe products as client projects unless explicitly provided later.
- Do not invent case studies, clients, revenue results, adoption numbers, or testimonials.
- Do not frame the sports application as a betting prediction engine.
- Do not guarantee predictions, profit, betting outcomes, or model accuracy.
- Do not expose proprietary product strategy, private prompts, scoring rules, or unreleased monetization details.
- Do not make employer related claims beyond high level and non sensitive context.

## Product Truth

The products should be positioned as evidence of engineering capability. They show how Jera Technologies designs structured workflows, builds usable product experiences, and turns analysis into reviewable outputs.

## Employer Related Work

Any reference to prior or current employer related work should stay high level. Focus on general capabilities such as enterprise modernization, authentication, integrations, process automation, and software delivery. Do not include sensitive systems, private business details, internal architecture, or proprietary implementation specifics.

## Current Website Boundary Check

The current site does not claim paid client work. It describes solution examples as patterns and product directions. The Structured Analysis Pipeline and the Matchup Analyzer are described as Jera built products and product portfolio entries.

The Matchup Analyzer page avoids claims about guaranteed outcomes, profit, betting outcomes, or model accuracy. It is presented as a decision support application for structured sports matchup analysis.
